Marta Betoldi

Marta Betoldi is an Argentine actress, screenwriter, and playwright known for her work in television, theater, and film. She has written for series such as Socias, Ciega a citas, Cuando me Sonreís, Daños Colaterales, Edha, Esperanza mía, Solamente vos, Las Estrellas, El Host II, La Villa, Planners II, and Escribiendo el Fin del Mundo.

Currently, she is the head writer for the adaptation of Fernando Gaitán's original series "Betty la Fea 20 años después" for Amazon Global. Additionally, she is developing an original series for Netflix LATAM, serving as the Content Development Coordinator for Gosua España, providing script consultancy for Hispanic-American production companies, and serving as a member of the jury for the International Emmy Awards.

Theater

Adapter of 'El cuento de la Mujer del Alergista' by Charles Busch (2011). This work premiered in Argentina at the Metropolitan Theater in a freely adapted version by the author.

"Adapter of 'Locos de contentos, muy Locos' by Jacobo Lagsner in a freely adapted version by the author. Winner of the Estrella de Mar Award, it premiered in Buenos Aires (2002)

Author of "Contracciones", directed by Leonor Manso, premiered at Sala El Nudo, as part of the Teatroxlaidentidad cycle, which she actively supported alongside other artists to advocate for the work of Abuelas de Plaza de Mayo (2001/2002). The play was later re-staged in its complete version outside the cycle under the direction of Mario Pasik (Teatro Andamio) (2004). It embarked on a full tour of Spain, spending two months at the Teatro de La Villa in Madrid. The play was selected to participate in the Festival Teatro a Mil in Chile, the Festival de Cádiz in Spain, and the Festival de Miami in the USA (2005). It was translated into 4 languages and premiered in Paris, London, Madrid, Mexico, and the USA. The play was published as part of the Teatroxlaidentidad cycle.

Author of "Cesárea", directed by Luis Luque, premiered at Sala Apolo in Buenos Aires, also part of the Teatroxlaidentidad cycle in 2001 and published by Editorial Eudeba.

Author of "Extras", a play awarded with a Special Mention by the National Institute of Theater of Buenos Aires (2000).

Teaching

Since 2005, she has been invited to teach various workshops at film Universities.

She conducts, alongside Fernando Castets, workshops on audiovisual writing for industry professionals and beginners.

It is worth noting that she has a catalog of original series, both solely authored and co-created with renowned authors in the industry such as David Serrano, Fernando Castets, Julio Rojas, Paula del Fierro, and Esteban del Campo.
